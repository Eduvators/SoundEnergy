Sound Energy Transformation

In electrical circuits, loads consume electrical energy and convert it into various forms of energy. One important transformation is the conversion to sound energy.

Electrical energy can be converted into sound waves through vibration of components.

Example: When an electric signal is sent to a speaker, it causes a diaphragm to vibrate, producing sound waves that we can hear. This principle is utilized in vehicle infotainment systems, warning buzzers, and horns to provide audio feedback and entertainment.
